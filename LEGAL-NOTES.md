# Project IRONLOOP Legal Notes (U.S.-focused)

This file is a practical risk guide for contributors. It is not legal advice.

## 1) Project Positioning

Project IRONLOOP is a bench Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) research platform.
It is intended for controlled testing, education, and software development.

## 2) Trademark and Branding

- Bosch, ME7, and related marks belong to their owners.
- Do not imply endorsement, partnership, or affiliation.
- Do not use Bosch logos as project branding.

Suggested footer language:
`Project IRONLOOP is an independent research project and is not affiliated with or endorsed by Bosch.`

## 3) Copyright / IP Boundaries

- Do not commit or distribute proprietary OEM firmware binaries unless you have explicit rights.
- Do not commit leaked/confidential service docs.
- Keep repository code and docs original or properly licensed.

## 4) Emissions / Regulatory Risk

- Do not market this project as a way to defeat emissions controls on road vehicles.
- Keep examples and docs focused on bench simulation and engineering research.
- Avoid publishing "delete" workflows or road-use tampering instructions.

## 5) Safety and Use Limits

- Bench use only.
- No direct recommendation for public-road deployment.
- Keep clear warnings in docs when discussing ECU calibration/testing.

## 6) Contributor Checklist

Before opening a PR, confirm:

- No proprietary binaries/docs were added.
- No trademark misuse or implied affiliation.
- No emissions-defeat marketing language.
- Bench-only framing remains clear.
